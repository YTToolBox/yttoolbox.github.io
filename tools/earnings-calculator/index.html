The Reality of YouTube Earnings: CPM vs RPM

Most online calculators give you fake, inflated numbers because they apply USA-based earnings to every channel. The YT ToolBox Earnings Analyzer is built differently. It uses an auto-detection algorithm to scan the channel's location and content type to provide realistic numbers.

How Geography Affects Ad Revenue

Advertisers bid differently based on the purchasing power of an audience. If a channel's primary audience is in the USA, UK, or Australia, their RPM (Revenue Per Mille) can range from $2.00 to $10.00. However, if the channel is based in India (IN) or Southeast Asia, the RPM drops significantly to $0.15 - $0.50. Our tool detects this and adjusts the math automatically.

The "YouTube Shorts" Trap

Many creators have billions of views but make very little money. Why? Because they upload YouTube Shorts. Shorts revenue is pooled and split, resulting in an RPM of roughly $0.01 to $0.05 per 1,000 views. If our engine detects that a video is a Short, it applies this micro-RPM to ensure you don't see fake "Million Dollar" estimates.
